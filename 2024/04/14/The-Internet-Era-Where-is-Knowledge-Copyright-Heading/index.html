The Internet Era: Where is Knowledge Copyright Heading

Author: Jask Don Date: April 14, 2024  14:01:48 Category: Web3

Preface

With the rapid development and widespread adoption of the Internet, the ways in which knowledge, information, and content are disseminated are undergoing profound changes. This transformation not only poses new challenges and issues for knowledge copyright but also provides unprecedented opportunities for the dissemination and sharing of knowledge. Against this backdrop, the rise of artificial intelligence has become a focal point of attention for creators and intellectual property rights holders. However, while artificial intelligence has its unique advantages and promising applications, it also presents certain limitations and challenges. This article will begin with an exploration of artificial intelligence, introducing the solutions and applications of the Story Protocol.

Opportunities in the Age of Artificial Intelligence

Creative Design with Generative AI

Generative AI, such as Midjourney, utilizes advanced algorithms and models to generate images and designs that meet user expectations based on provided descriptions. Here are some specific application scenarios:

  1. Personalized Design and Creative Generation: Generative AI can quickly produce images, illustrations, or designs that meet user expectations based on their descriptions and requirements, significantly improving the efficiency and quality of personalized design.
  2. Artistic Creation and Innovation: Artists and designers can utilize generative AI for artistic creation, exploring and experimenting with new artistic styles and forms of expression.
  3. Product Design and Simulation: In the process of product design and development, generative AI can assist designers and engineers in rapidly generating and simulating design proposals and effects, accelerating the product development cycle.

Document Processing and Creation with Text-based AI

Text-based AI, such as ChatGPT-4.0, possesses powerful natural language processing capabilities, allowing it to derive, adapt, and summarize content based on user-provided documents, significantly enhancing the efficiency and quality of document processing and creation. Here are some specific application scenarios:

  1. Document Derivation and Adaptation: Text-based AI can generate new content and versions based on existing documents and materials, providing creators with more inspiration and options.
  2. Document Summarization and Extraction: When dealing with a large volume of documents and materials, text-based AI can automatically summarize and extract key information and viewpoints, helping users quickly access and understand the content.
  3. Writing Assistant and Editing Tools: Text-based AI can serve as a writing assistant and editing tool, offering writing suggestions, grammar checks, and content optimization, assisting users in improving writing quality and efficiency.

Challenges in the Age of Artificial Intelligence

With the rapid development and widespread adoption of artificial intelligence technology, especially the extensive application of generative AI in the fields of creation and design, intellectual property issues are becoming increasingly prominent. The production capacity of AI is influenced by large models, and there are numerous similar or identical AI models on the market, posing serious challenges to the intellectual property rights of creators.

Issues of Model Sharing and Misuse

Model Misuse and Repetitive Use: Due to the existence of numerous identical or similar AI models on the market, models created by creators can easily be misused or reused by others. For example, a work created by creator A on a certain platform is transformed into an NFT and uploaded to OpenSea, then purchased by user B and resold at a higher price on OpenSea. At this point, user C might screenshot and save the NFT and mint it into their own NFT, which seriously infringes upon the intellectual property rights of the creator.

NFT and Intellectual Property Protection

Authenticity and Uniqueness of NFTs: While NFT technology can provide unique identity and ownership proof for digital artworks, there still exist issues of NFTs being copied, counterfeited, or misused in practical applications. Ensuring the authenticity and uniqueness of NFTs, as well as effectively protecting the intellectual property rights of creators, is an urgent problem that needs to be addressed.

All the aforementioned issues are challenges we are currently facing in the Internet era. So, how can we address these problems?

Blockchain technology may be the best solution to these issues. Due to its characteristics of decentralization, immutability, and transparency, blockchain technology can provide a more secure, transparent, and reliable protection mechanism for intellectual property rights.

Story Protocol could potentially become the ultimate weapon to solve this problem

Company-Level IP

Registering a single NFT obviously cannot meet the needs of large clients. Below is a comparison of the registration processes:

Individual Registering IP

  • Contract Address
  • Token ID

Company Registering IP

  • Contract Address

It is clear that individual registration of IP requires providing a Token ID, and individuals generally do not have the time or technical expertise to create separate contracts for their IP. This may expose individual IP to risks. Although individuals may not mind, large companies definitely would.

Therefore, company-level IP registration becomes a solution to such issues. Companies can easily create their own smart contracts and register them as IPAs, and all NFTs under this contract belong to the company’s IP assets.

The benefits of this approach are also reflected in the dispute module. Using this model, SP can easily handle IP disputes between large clients, reducing arbitration costs.
